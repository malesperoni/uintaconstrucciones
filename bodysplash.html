Rebranding Body Splash

I worked on the 2026 rebranding of CHER’s three body splashes, redefining color palettes, typography, and finishes. The goal was to create a cohesive yet distinctive collection, enhancing brand identity and visual consistency across the line.

The CHER logotype was refined with increased weight to connect with a younger audience. The numeric element was reduced and integrated into the dot, ensuring clarity and usability in small-scale logo applications.

The bottle was redesigned with softer, rounded proportions to better suit a shorter and wider format, improving ergonomics and comfort when carried.

The color palette was aligned with the original perfumes to ensure immediate visual association between the body splash and its corresponding fragrance. A linear gradient fading into transparency allows the liquid inside to remain visible, while the vertical typography enhances brand legibility on retail displays.

The visual system was designed to work consistently across digital and physical applications. By aligning proportions, color usage, and typographic hierarchy, the brand achieves a cohesive presence while maintaining flexibility across different formats and scales.
